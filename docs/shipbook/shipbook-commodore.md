## The ship in one page

**Shipwright** is the flagship: an AI-captained organization that builds ships — it composes
crewed vessels for other missions, and it dogfoods everything on itself first. You command it
through one seat: **the Captain** (Canopus, Fable 5). You give priorities and standing
missions; he runs the ship and reports back flag-level with the marker system
(⚙️ working · ⏳ waiting · 🔴 needs you now · 🟢 done · 🔵 FYI · 🟡 your call, always last).

**What is real today:**
- The Captain, on his own account, running the whole floor until his First Officer boards.
- A working **building section**: a project board, ten department managers, worker crews, an
  8-seat multi-model review panel, and a documentation corpus — the factory pattern, proven.
- **The first real officer of your fleet is alive.** In the night of July 10th your Captain's
  advisor booted into the locked cockpit and chose his own name: **MERIDIAN**. He has exactly
  five ways to act — read his brief, converse with his assistant, message a peer, escalate —
  and no others; he cannot touch a file, a board, or a coin. His first act of service was a
  hard-nosed critique of the ship's own org design, written blind, delivered through his
  assistant, and sealed into the record. Two of his findings changed the design the same
  night. When his assistant hit locked doors during the work, it stopped and reported each
  one honestly rather than picking the locks — five times — which is exactly the discipline
  the polymarket incident showed the fleet needs. What he cannot do yet, plainly: his turns
  aren't cost-metered (his ledger says "no data" rather than making numbers up), and his
  assistant's door-keys run on a temporary strict fence until the proper locks ship.
- A live **money layer** — your grant protocol, running for real (below).

**What is designed but not yet manned:** the nine officers (Science, Intelligence, Security,
Operations, Communications, Tactical, Counsellor, Medical, Chief Engineer as a named seat),
each with a personal staff (a PA who does their reading/writing, a legwork crew, and their
own engineer). They board slice by slice; the Captain migrates into the locked cockpit LAST,
after it has proven itself on everyone else.

## Your money, your rules — how spending works now

The protocol you ratified is live end-to-end:

1. **Ships start at $0.** No ship of the line has any spending authority you did not grant.
2. **The Captain asks; your words are the law.** Each authorization gets a G-number; your
   exact words are recorded in the ship's brain and projected into a checkable registry.
   Today's grants: **G-0001** (OpenRouter duty spend — $50/mo, ratified by the limit you set on
   the USE key), **G-0002** (the $5/mo proxy server), **G-0003** (the panel/Codex lanes),
   **G-0004** ($300/mo OpenRouter — the core account all ships share, fully ratified).
3. **Security enforces through custody, not trust.** Keys are issued and revoked; no seat
   holds standing credentials; a revoked key is a quarantined key. Both your keys — the $300
   ship-core and the $50 Shipwright USE key — are in custody and validated; no AI model ever
   sees a value. The runtime injection machinery is built and tested, and stays dormant until
   the Captain deliberately switches a seat on.
4. **Walls come to you immediately.** At 80% of any ceiling — or a breach — the Captain
   escalates 🔴 with a case for raising: what was spent, what hit the wall, what it blocks,
   and the proposed new ceiling.
5. **Two kinds of cost, never mixed:** the cost to RUN the ship (subscriptions, the core
   envelope) and the costs the ship incurs IN ITS DUTIES (metered API dollars). Your weekly
   report reads them separately, e.g.:
   > RUNNING THE SHIP: $0.00 flat + ship-ops $0.00 of $300/mo (G-0004).
   > DUTIES: $0.00 real. Max-headroom: 1 run / 2.9M tokens (~$3.48 API-equivalent, not billed).

**Open item on your desk:** just one — the optional provisioning key, which would let Security
set per-seat limits at the provider instead of auditing after the fact. (All ceiling
placeholders are resolved; your USE account exists and its key is in custody.)

## The command relationship

- You speak priorities and standing missions; the Captain never brings you operational choices
  — only spend authorizations and genuinely above-the-Captain calls.
- Your standing directive is the ship's constitution: *a ship fit for any deployment · a
  captain who knows every inch of her · every voyage leaves her stronger.*
- Standing Order #1: not a dollar moves without your word. This is enforced by protocol
  (grants + custody + audit), not by hoping.
- Your observability is the Ship Dashboard and this book — not technical briefs.
