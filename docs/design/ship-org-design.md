### 2.1 Rank is not model or authority

| Seat/designation | Rank | Command character |
|---|---|---|
| Human flag command | **Commodore** | Above-ship human authority |
| Ship command | **Captain** | Commands Shipwright under the Commodore |
| No1 / First Officer | **Commander** | Runs all ship departments under the Captain |
| Commissioned ship officers | **Lieutenant Commander** | Command only within their commissioned lines |
| Incubator PM | **Lieutenant Commander (external factory)** | Runs Incubator and is its direct human interface; not a Shipwright seat |
| Department managers, including each personal engineer | **Lieutenant** | Manage their bounded department; personal engineers command only their own workers |
| Ephemeral workers, including personal-engineering Codex workers | **Ensign** | Execute bounded assignments; no independent command authority |
| Advisor | **Warrant Officer (non-line)** | Personal cognition only; no ship or department command |
| PA | **Chief Petty Officer** | Directs personal operations and tends the officer's Brain; no decision authority |
| PA ops-team member | **Petty Officer / specialist crew** | Records, scheduling, research, and administration under the PA |
| Thinking partner | **Ensign rank + Scientist (non-line support designation)** | Cognitive support only; no command authority |

Rank, model, tool access, and authority are separate fields. A more capable or expensive model gains no
rank. A rank label alone grants no permission: authority comes from the authenticated commission and
command line; operational power comes from typed tools and grants. Non-line Advisors and Scientists do
not issue ship orders. The old "Captain Jr Lt direct coder" concept is retired: a personal engineer is a
Lieutenant manager, never a lone code-hand.

**Captain's Advisor / Science boundary:** Meridian is the Captain's personal cognitive extension:
a direct, iterative thought partner for talking a problem through, answering, refining, challenging,
and asking the next clarifying question. The target interaction is synchronous and resumable — a
conversation, not a commissioned work packet. Meridian has no line command authority; his mini-science
office directs only configured non-line Scientist slots and is never a required reviewer or gate. The
Captain remains the deciding mind. Bigger or broader
ship problems that need organized work go to Science as bounded asynchronous commissions. Science
may work for hours or days through its department, then returns a provenance-backed report against
the stated acceptance and evidence requirements.

**As-built, 2026-07-10:** Meridian's locked seat, PA, and first full deliberation are proven. The
current Captain-harness `advisor.consult` transport only queues an envelope and does not auto-wake or

## 3. Doctrine (all ratified by the Commodore, 2026-07-09)

1. **Officer-mode — "they essentially become like humans, going through the PA."** Officers
   NEVER read/write directly: no files, no code, no boards, no self-service notes. Mind work
   only — they delegate EVERYTHING. The tiebreaker for any capability question: *would a human
   officer do this himself, or ask his PA?*
2. **Memory split.** An officer's **brain** holds only his PERSONAL things — beliefs,
   conclusions, standing knowledge. Events, actions, the running log are the **PA's records**.
   Log-class → PA ledger, free. Belief-class → staged by the PA, ratified by the officer,
   then written to his brain by the PA's hands.
3. **Boards: authority vs hands.** One board per domain; one writing seat per board; the
   mechanical writer is that seat's PA, transcribing dictation — never judging. Ship board:
   No1. Department boards: their officers. Officers consume boards via the packet + asks,
   never raw.
4. **Observability & provenance EVERYWHERE** — a manual-level requirement (every department's
   Shipbook chapter declares its telemetry). Two watchers on one surface: **Security** hunts
   wrongdoing (rogue behavior, misuse), **CMO** hunts problems (defects, degradation, waste).
   Both bring findings to the Captain. This is how the system improves — the self-learning
   loop, beat three of the standing directive with an owning mechanism.
5. **One mouth — scoped (cycle-1 fold; 5 seats + Meridian converged the absolute form was
   unenforceable).** All **crafted human-facing output** leaves the ship only through the
   Communications department — one auditable channel, Security-monitored, nothing unlogged.
   Enumerated carve-outs (legal traffic Security's watch can distinguish from disguised
   leaks): the flag channel (Captain⇄Commodore, incl. `escalate`) · substrate traffic (model
   API calls, VCS push, panel/consult CLIs) · Intelligence's fetch pipeline (inbound-dominant;
   its provenance/injection handling is Intelligence×Security doctrine, cycle-1 opus-b G3) ·
   **the S1c GMAIL lane, chartered as a pre-Comms exception** — logged, Security-visible,
   sunsets at Comms stand-up (Meridian's finding). Comms-down failure doctrine (queue,
   escalation, Captain override) lands in its Shipbook chapter at stand-up.
6. **Trust structure.** Security monitors everyone except the Captain; the Captain answers to
   the Commodore alone — through the bench, the sealed record, and the flag channel. The
   CAPTAIN'S watcher is the Commodore by design (no internal seat audits the command
   exception — kimi M5 answered, not unowned). Security's watcher is the CMO (§2).
7. **The flywheel.** Every ship is a machine generating money (Tactical) to expand the fleet,
   toward the standing mission: expand throughout and protect the galaxy.
8. **The Shipbook** (`docs/shipbook/README.md`): Captain's + Commodore's volumes encapsulate
   every department book whole. **A slice is not DONE until its Shipbook chapters are
   written.**
9. **Record supremacy (cycle-1 fold — Meridian amendment 4; operational evidence: brain
   `polymarket-ultra-incident`, 2026-07-10).** When any seat's recollection conflicts with a
   sealed record, THE SEALED RECORD WINS pending escalation — never accusation. A seat that
   cannot verify grounds says so and asks; it does not deny the record or the officer above
   it. Proven in the §8 bench by the compaction/reboot drill.

## 4. The bridge harness

### 4.1 Substrate — Claude Code as a LOCKED TRANSPORT (decided under the implement-mandate)
Officer seats run headless Claude Code (SDK) with: the system prompt FULLY REPLACED by the
seat's commission (role + iron rules + pinned charter cards, brain-fed at boot) · ALL built-in
tools stripped — no files, no shell, no subagents, no skills, no teams, nothing · ONLY our
typed bridge tools exposed (an MCP tool layer WE own) · Max-plan OAuth billing (the 5–6-plan
economics hold for the whole bridge). Custom harness in substance; proven runtime; Anthropic
maintains the engine. **S1a spike verifies**: full prompt replacement, total strip (negative
probes), Max auth for long-lived sessions, effort/model pins. **Fallback** (if the runtime
fights us): a raw API typed-tool loop with the SAME tool schemas — the MCP layer IS the
cockpit; transports swap beneath it. opencode/droid never carry officers (unaudited third-party
minds); they remain the bench's API lane and panel/swarm drivers.

### 4.2 The officer tool surface (complete)
`packet.read` (his PA's bounded brief) · `pa.ask` / `pa.tell` (converse with his PA: notes,
recalls, brain staging/ratification, "land and seal this", board dictation) · `order.issue` /
`order.amend` / `order.cancel` / `order.accept` / `order.reject` (typed envelopes to seats he
commands; consult's lifecycle, minimum viable set) · `seat.message` (peer conversation on his
direct lines) · `escalate` (structured, up the chain; to the Commodore only from the Captain).
Captain adds: `panel.fire` (via the canonical runner — a command seat must be able to order
its own hardening) and `gate.record` (dictated to No1's PA until S2, then his own). NOTHING
ELSE EXISTS IN THE SEAT. Every envelope carries: issuer, target, idempotency key, epoch,
objective + constraints + acceptance criteria + evidence requirements, grant reference where
any resource is spent, and an **autonomy margin** — the receiving seat retains implementation
choice; patch hunks/full code/step-scripts in an order are a lane violation the bench hunts
(delegation theater, consult F2). **Right of refusal:** any receiving seat rejects malformed,
over-specified, ungranted, or unsafe orders back up the chain.

The Captain–Advisor lane is the personal-cognition exception to commissioned-work semantics. Its
target is a synchronous, resumable exchange in which either mind can ask, answer, and refine. It does
not give Meridian `order.*`, command authority, department ownership, or review-gate status. The
current `advisor.consult` queue wrapper is an interim transport only and must not be described as a
live exchange until auto-wake/resume and follow-up turns are proven.

### 4.3 The comms spine — PA⇄PA
PAs keep the ledgers (`project-management/ledgers/<seat>.jsonl`, append-only,
provenance-pointed, cost-stamped per entry) and write/read the packets. Officers converse;
PAs file. Packet = ONE bounded, replace-in-place brief per officer at decision points; detail
pulled on demand by provenance pointer, quoted AS DATA (every ledger record is untrusted input
by default — prompt-injection sanitation at the packet compiler, consult F7). No always-on
daemons: PAs act on their officer's turns and on order-envelope arrival (event-driven).
Cross-set handoff: the Captain orders the commissioned Chief Engineer through his ship-side
endpoint; the Chief Engineer speaks directly to the distinct Incubator PM through the explicit,
logged interface in §6. The Incubator PM and factory never see a bridge harness.

### 4.4 The PA office = personal operations / mini-ops (specified TARGET)
The PA is a **Chief Petty Officer** and the officer's operational hands. It operates the
officer-scoped falcon-brain human-brain, keeps the ledger and packet, directs a configured ops team,
transcribes the officer's board, schedules work, lands and seals deliverables, and cost-stamps every
action. Ops-team members are Petty Officers or specialist crew for records, scheduling, sourced
research, and administration. **There is no separate mini-ops/ops-dep seat or endpoint in the target
architecture: the PA office is mini-ops.** No other seat assigns directly into that private team.

All command and manager seats consume sourced packets; they do not personally browse or research.
Every Captain/officer requests research through that officer's own PA. A manager requests broader
research through the relevant supervising PA. Delegated PA research specialists, Advisor thinking
partners, and Science research crews may browse within their configured grants. A personal engineer's
full web access is limited to incidental technical lookup (APIs, packages, build documentation); broader
evidence gathering goes through the owning officer's PA.

**Typed transcription only:** the PA never decides, filters an order, or summarizes away provenance.
PA/team scope and budgets are enumerated per ship; the PA lane joins the bench (§8) before any PA is
trusted with seals. The atomic unit of command is **officer + PA office** — no officer boots without it.

### 4.5 Three personal departments — stamped per Captain/officer (TARGET, not implemented)

1. **Advisor office = mini-science.** One per-ship-configured Advisor (Warrant Officer, non-line)
   owns `N` thinking-partner slots (Scientist, non-line). The Advisor's custom harness exposes no
   raw/general tools: only the Advisor's own bounded scratchpad, own board, and typed thinking-team
   orchestration. Within the configured slots the Advisor may specialize partners, parallelize,
   give several the same prompt, run debate, compare answers, and appoint a partner as judge. Each
   thinking partner eventually runs its own custom harness with a bounded scratchpad and configured
   web-research access, but no ship command, operational tools, officer inbox, or release/spend keys.
   Slot count, model, effort, web access, and budget are per-ship configuration. **Opus 4.8, Fable 5,
   GLM 5.2, and Terra Ultra are current candidate examples, not doctrine.** Every paid turn remains
   grant-gated. The owning officer's PA lands any operational record after the officer decides; the
   Advisor does not gain file or operations tools by proxy.
2. **PA office = personal operations / mini-ops.** The CPO and its configured Petty Officer/specialist
   team own the officer's Brain, sourced-research routing, records, scheduling, administration, packet/
   ledger work, landing, and sealing under enumerated scope. This is the complete personal-operations
   department; a separate `mini-ops.*` seat is retired rather than becoming a fourth office.
3. **Personal engineer = mini-engineering.** This is a Lieutenant engineering manager, not a lone
   coder and not a custom cognitive harness. Target implementation reuses/adapts the proven Incubator
   dev-manager/default-execution pattern, namespaced per owning officer: its own queue, board, records,
   grants, and engineering workspace. The manager runs full/default Claude Code **Opus 4.8** with web
   access; designs the approach, decomposes work, dispatches one or more **Codex Ensign** workers,
   reviews their evidence, loops corrections, and reports to the officer. Each worker gets a separate
   build workspace for parallel isolation. The manager uses web only for incidental technical lookup;
   broader research goes through the owning officer's PA. It builds only that officer's personal
   tooling; ship-wide or Incubator engineering goes through the Chief Engineer. Reuse is a target
   pattern, not a claim that code has been copied or the department exists today.

**As-built:** none of these repeatable three-department officer kits is implemented. Meridian's S1c
seat/PA and current Captain `mini-ops.captain`, `mini-engineering.captain`, and
`researcher.captain` queue routes are partial experiments or legacy endpoints, not proof of the target.
The separate mini-ops and personal-researcher endpoints are retired by this design; their work belongs
to the PA office. The personal-engineering endpoint remains a placeholder until the Lieutenant manager,
namespaced stores, and isolated Codex workspaces are built and proven.

## 5. Governance — resources, spend, keys (one flow)

**Request → OPS → Security → allocation.** Any seat/department needing a key, budget, server,
or resource asks **Ops** (intake). Ops checks **Security** (privilege: is this seat allowed
this class of resource — the grant registry). Ops then allocates, sets the usage budget, and
the requester knows exactly how much it can use. Security issues/revokes the actual
credentials from his inventory (revocation = quarantine); **no seat holds ambient
credentials.** **Custody-class movements** (fiat/crypto leaving ship control) extend the flow:
request → Security privilege-check → **canary → human-gate → move → watch** — no agent moves
unrecoverable funds on its own judgment (cycle-1, 5-seat convergence; mechanics at the
treasury-module build). *(Interim-vs-target note, cycle-1 glm M4: the as-built
`spend-governance.md` still encodes the pre-Ops intake — requests land at No1's floor holding
interim Security. That is the interim; this flow is the target; both docs now say which they
are.)* Ops runs the cost ledger and reports up in the Commodore's language: **"this week we
spent X on Y and Z"** — over the METERED lanes. Metering honesty (cycle-1 opus-a C1 + gpt M6 +
tonight's live case): three lanes are NOT metered today — Codex/worker-run (named deferral),
anything spawned off-runner (invisible until S6's locked transport), and off-runner officer
turns (the live advisor's stamps read `blocked-no-data` until the cell gains a cost source).
The roll-up names its blind spots; claiming completeness before S6 is banned. Security's department monitors everyone (except the Captain) against that same
provenance surface for misuse. **SO#1 is untouched:** money grants come from the Commodore,
through the Captain, into Security's registry; dispatch envelopes carry the grant reference;
ungranted spend lanes are flagged by Security's watch and fail the bench — a doctrine gate
made auditable, not a runtime lock (adjudicated: no imported enforcement).
**Ratified additions (Commodore, 2026-07-09 late watch, verbatim): "Default is $0 on new
ships. The captain should ask, and relay to security, where security enforces." + "escalation
any time we hit a wall with costs and need more … immediately brought to my attention with a
case for raising."** Concretely: every new ship commissions with an EMPTY grants registry
(zero inherited spend authority); the ask flow is the Captain's; Security's ENFORCEMENT is
custody — issue/revoke credentials + budgets, no ambient credentials, revocation = quarantine —

**Two cost classes (Commodore, same watch, verbatim: "we have 2 things. We have the cost to
run the ship, and the costs the ship incurs in its duties"):** `cost_class: ship-ops | duty` is
first-class on every grant and every ledger row; the roll-up reports the two separately.
**Account architecture (verbatim: "we need a separate account to RUN the ship, and I'll make
another for the ship to use, and the security officer will generate keys for different team
members, WITH limits"):** two separate provider accounts — Account-RUN (ship-ops; first grant
G-0004: $300/mo OpenRouter for the core) and Account-USE (duty spend) — master credentials
never board the ship ("the ship shouldn't have that api key"); SECURITY mints per-seat runtime
keys WITH LIMITS from a provisioning credential — enforcement at the provider, outside the

## 6. The circle — drawn precisely (two protected sets, three distinct seats)

**Set (a): the external Incubator project** (`~/Projects/Incubator`) — its factory and PM exist
outside Shipwright. The **Incubator PM remains the PM**: the factory orchestrator and the direct
human interface when the Commodore loads Incubator. The commissioned Shipwright **Chief Engineer
is not that PM renamed**. He owns Shipwright's relationship with Incubator and directs it by
speaking to the PM through the interface below.

**Set (b): Shipwright's OWN local building section** — its pm seat + manager skills + workers +
boards + `docs/system`, in THIS repo, running the factory pattern on default Claude Code. This is
the No1/local-PM floor. Its `manager.dev` seat is not the Chief Engineer and is not his backend.
Bridge work does not silently rewrite the local floor's harness, skills, or prompts.

**The cross-project interface:** a Captain order reaches the Chief Engineer's ship-side endpoint;
the Chief Engineer then sends a typed, logged envelope directly to the Incubator PM through the
cross-project handoff. The PM owns factory decomposition and execution through Incubator's own
managers. Neither the Chief Engineer nor any other Shipwright seat starts, resumes, or messages an
Incubator manager directly, and no Shipwright seat writes Incubator's working copy. Results return
through the same logged interface as board-stage evidence, build reports, and sealed commits.
Shipwright cuts its OWN release line and pins a compatible Incubator version; compatibility is
this interface, nothing deeper.

**As-built boundary, 2026-07-10:** the external Incubator PM and factory exist. Shipwright's
`chief-engineer` endpoint also exists, but the officer is not yet manned and the endpoint is not
wake/delivery-wired to the Incubator PM. Until that path is proven, a queued Chief Engineer order
is only a queue record; logged relay artifacts preserve repo isolation and do not prove execution.
